Navajo Verb Parser Help

Just type in a Navajo verb in the input box given on the Main page. Make sure it is one of the supported verbs listed on this page.

But I don't know Navajo

If you don't know Navajo and just want to see the parser work, try the following inputs:

  • naashneH
  • yishbeHeHzh
  • yicha

The output may not make much sense unless you understand at least a little bit about Navajo verbs. My paper contains a short introduction of them. Look for the Navajo Verb section.

Diacritics and Special Characters

In the text box you may use the following key combinations to generate the corresponding characters. If a given vowel is both nasalized and high, apply the accent first, then the nasal hook.

  • Alt-1 for ɫ
  • Alt-2 for ó (the accent only applies to vowels)
  • Alt-3 for oͅ (nasals only apply to vowels)

You must use Firefox, Netscape 7, or Mozilla in order for the diacritic marking facility to work.

If the diacritic and Unicode facilities do not work on your browser, you may instead use the following sequence of characters in their place:

  • H for high tone
  • N for nasal
  • HN for high nasal
  • lB for barred-l

If you have a long high/nasal vowel, place the H/N/HN after each vowel in the long vowel sequence. For example, naashné would be rendered naashneH, and shiɫbéézh can be represented with shilBbeHeHzh.

Note that if a vowel is both high and nasalized, the H should come first followed by the N after the vowel.
